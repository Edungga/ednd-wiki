The Merchant Guilds are not a single organization, but a powerful coalition of chartered guilds, trade houses, craftsmen's leagues, and mercantile associations that operate throughout the realm.

Where nobles squabble, priests preach, and cities crumble, the Guilds continue to move grain, iron, wool, timber, salt, and silver from one corner of the realm to another.

While individual guilds compete fiercely, all are bound by a common charter known as The Fulcrum.

At the head of the coalition stands the Dean of Guilds. An appointed role elected by the guild masters by popular vote. They reside in the [[Harrow's Keep#Hall of Scales|Hall of Scales in Harrow's Keep]], a vast stone guildhall overlooking the markets.

# Major Guilds

## The Iron Consortium
Deals in mines, foundries, smiting, and the metal trade.
Holds a particular influence in [[Troll King's Rest]] and [[Irongate]]

## The Golden Trust
Bankers, lenders, accountants, and investors. The Trust insists it merely manages money, few believe that.

## The Wayfaring Company
Operators of caravan routes, inns, couriers and post. Without them, much of the realm would be totally isolated.

## The Salt Guild
Controls coastal saltworks and preservation trade. Food shortages often make them more influential than local rulers.
Holds a particular influence in [[Hollow Harbour]] and [[Caer Thalos]] / much of [[The Mourning Isles]]

# The Fulcrum
The Guilds Charter. Every guild swears adherence to the Fulcrum.

Though hundreds of pages long, its core principles are simple:
- **Honest Weight**. A merchant's scales must not be falsified
- **Safe Passage**. Guild caravans are entitled to protection and neutrality whenever possible.
- **Contract Above Pride**. Written agreements are considered sacred.
- **No Guild Wars**. Competition is permitted, violence between guilds is not.
- **Preserve the Roads**. Trade routes, bridges, and waystations must be maintained.
- **The Realm Before Profit**. No guild may knowingly unleash supernatural threats, spread plague, or destabilize trade regions for personal gain.
	- This final rule is violated far more often than the Guilds publicly admit.

There are members, and even entire guilds, that take the charter a bit too seriously and have begun considering it a sacred text, taking the Fulcrum to a religious extent
